# Adulting Lab VinCore Poster Design

## Purpose

Create a new A0 portrait infographic poster for a proposed VinCore course:

> **ADULTING LAB**  
> A New VinCore Course for Real-Life Survival Skills

The poster should communicate that university students need practical life
skills beyond academic knowledge, and that `Adulting Lab` is a workshop-based
course designed to build those skills.

## Audience

The audience is a mixed university audience: students, faculty, VinCore
reviewers, and non-specialists. The poster should feel approachable, student
friendly, and easy to understand at a glance.

## Source Content

Use the user's supplied content as the authoritative source:

- Objective Finding
- Fact Finding
- Problem Finding
- Idea Finding
- Solution Finding
- Acceptance Finding
- Final Recommendation

Do not invent new course claims or institutional approval status. Present the
course as a proposal.

## Visual Reference

Use the supplied screenshot as the visual mood reference:

- Pastel academic/infographic style.
- Thick black rounded borders.
- Large white content cards.
- Soft drop-shadow or offset colored backing.
- Friendly doodle-style illustrations.
- Light grid-paper or notebook background in selected areas.
- Clear numbered or labeled sections.

Adapt the visual language to Adulting Lab instead of copying the environmental
poster. Replace plants/earth imagery with life-skills illustrations such as:

- wallet and coins;
- email/message bubble;
- CV or LinkedIn profile sheet;
- checklist/contract document;
- shield or lock for digital safety;
- toolkit folder for final project.

## Format And Deliverables

- A0 portrait poster.
- English only.
- Editable PowerPoint as the primary file.
- One-page PDF preview.
- PNG preview.

## Poster Narrative

### Hero

Use a large top hero card.

Main title:

```text
ADULTING LAB
```

Subtitle:

```text
A New VinCore Course for Real-Life Survival Skills
```

Supporting line:

```text
Helping students manage money, work, communication, and everyday decisions beyond the classroom.
```

The hero should include a friendly student-life illustration, preferably a
student holding a toolkit/folder surrounded by small icons for money, email,
career, documents, and safety.

### Six Finding Cards

Create six large cards using the Creative Problem Solving sequence:

1. `Objective Finding`
2. `Fact Finding`
3. `Problem Finding`
4. `Idea Finding`
5. `Solution Finding`
6. `Acceptance Finding`

Each card should have:

- a numbered pastel badge;
- a short heading;
- a concise claim;
- 2-4 supporting bullets or short phrases.

### Card Content

#### 1. Objective Finding

Goal:

```text
Design a VinCore course that builds practical life skills for university and early adulthood.
```

Desired outcome:

```text
Students become more independent, responsible, and prepared for real-world challenges.
```

#### 2. Fact Finding

Key observations:

- Students can perform well academically while struggling with daily
  responsibilities.
- Budgeting, professional communication, internships, and independent living
  are often learned too late.
- Real-life skills matter, but are not always directly taught in class.

Insight:

```text
University prepares students for exams, but not always for everyday adult decisions.
```

#### 3. Problem Finding

Core problem:

```text
Students lack practical skills needed to handle real-life situations with confidence.
```

Why it matters:

- Money mistakes.
- Unprofessional communication.
- Missed opportunities.
- Scam risks.
- Feeling overwhelmed by adult responsibilities.

Problem statement:

```text
How might we create a VinCore course that equips students with practical skills for life beyond academics?
```

#### 4. Idea Finding

Show possible course ideas as small chips or mini cards:

- Personal finance and budgeting.
- Professional email and workplace communication.
- CV, LinkedIn, and interview preparation.
- Time management and stress planning.
- Contract and document reading.
- Scam awareness and digital safety.
- Independent living and decision-making.

Selected direction:

```text
A practical, workshop-based course that teaches students how to handle real-life responsibilities step by step.
```

#### 5. Solution Finding

This should be the largest content section because it is the proposed course.

Course name:

```text
Adulting Lab: Money, Work, and Real-Life Survival Skills
```

Course modules:

1. `Money Basics` — Budgeting, saving, spending habits, and emergency planning.
2. `Professional Communication` — Email writing, meeting etiquette, asking for
   help, and networking.
3. `Career Readiness` — CV building, LinkedIn profiles, interview skills, and
   internship preparation.
4. `Real-Life Documents` — Understanding contracts, forms, rental agreements,
   and official documents.
5. `Digital Safety` — Recognizing scams, phishing, fake job offers, and privacy
   risks.
6. `Final Life Kit Project` — Students create a personal Adulting Toolkit.

Final Life Kit items:

- monthly budget;
- professional CV;
- sample email templates;
- contract checklist;
- personal action plan.

Why this solution works:

- Practical and immediately useful.
- Relevant to all students.
- Skill-based instead of theory-heavy.
- Closely connected to real student life.

#### 6. Acceptance Finding

Implementation:

- VinCore elective or first-year support course.
- Workshops, simulations, and real-life tasks.
- Practical assignments instead of traditional exams.

Expected outcomes:

- Students manage money more wisely.
- Students communicate more professionally.
- Students feel more confident with adult responsibilities.
- Students are better prepared for internships and independent life.

Long-term impact:

```text
Adulting Lab can bridge the gap between academic success and real-world readiness.
```

### Final Recommendation Box

Create a visually prominent bottom box.

Final proposal:

```text
We propose Adulting Lab as a new VinCore course that teaches students essential life skills often missing from traditional academic education.
```

Main value:

```text
Not just how to succeed in class — but how to succeed in life.
```

## Layout Direction

Use the approved playful infographic direction:

1. Hero card at the top.
2. Four medium cards in a two-column grid for Objective, Fact, Problem, and
   Idea.
3. A wider Solution card because course modules are the main substance.
4. An Acceptance card and Final Recommendation box near the bottom.
5. Small footer area for team/name/course info if provided later; otherwise use
   a simple neutral footer.

## Typography

- Use bold, friendly sans-serif headings.
- Make `ADULTING LAB` very large and readable from a distance.
- Keep body text concise and readable, not tiny.
- Use bullets, chips, and short phrases instead of long paragraphs.

## Color System

Use a warm, friendly pastel palette:

- Cream/off-white background.
- Black outlines.
- Pastel yellow for money and hero highlights.
- Pastel pink for problem/risk.
- Pastel blue for communication/career.
- Pastel green for confidence/growth.
- Small red/orange accents for warning/scam awareness.

## Quality Gates

Before delivery:

1. Verify A0 portrait size.
2. Verify PDF contains exactly one page.
3. Verify all six finding sections are present.
4. Verify the six course modules are present.
5. Verify the final recommendation and main value statement are present.
6. Verify wording presents Adulting Lab as a proposal, not as an approved
   existing course.
7. Verify the poster remains readable and not text-heavy.
8. Verify the PowerPoint remains editable.
